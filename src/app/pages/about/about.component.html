How to use the Application

Creating an account is simple. Fill out account credentials: Hub Id and Password. Your Hub Id should match your real Hub Id. Password can be anything you want it to be. Select three security questions and type the answers for each. This is done for account recovery, if you ever forget your password or need to change it. Then fill out your personal information. Please use your real name and email. We will not email you unless there is a major update, or if there is something account specific that we need to disclose. Review the Terms of Service and click the Register button.

In the event that you get completely locked out of your account, you will need to provide a copy of your Drivers License and PERNER for verification. If your information on your Drivers License does not match what is found on Roster, we will be unable to assist. Misuse of this site will result in disabling your account. If your account is disabled, you can appeal to have it re-enabled once. For any of the above assistance, please utilize the email link in the Contact page.

The main part of the application consists of 4 sections. Previous Week, Current Week Actual Hours, Current Week Schedule, Next Week Schedule. Enter Payrate if you want the application to approximate your pay. It will not be accurate. It uses a 5 day 40 hour work week to calculate pay and does not account for regular days off, incentive-pay, or double-time from the Bullpen. Clock in and out fields use military time. For example, shifts starting at 1:00pm must be entered as 13:00. Forms were made large on purpose for accessibility reasons.

Previous Week consists of your actual clock in and clock out times. The application will automatically calculate your hours for each day, including a lunch break for all shifts over 7 hours and 15 minutes.

Current week actual hours consists of your actual clock in and clock out for each day. The DOT hour calculation will consist of that day and the 7 days before it. If you go over 65 hours, a warning will display.

After entering your schedule in for current week, each day will calculate what you are scheduled for that day, and the previous 7 days actually worked hours. If you worked 62 hours in 7 days and are scheduled to work a 10 hour shift the next day, the application will display a warning that working that shift will put you over DOT hours.

Next week schedule will project hours from previous clocks in the current week and scheduled days of the next week.

Once you make changes to the form, you must use the Update button at the bottom for those changes to take effect. At the end of the week, select the New Week button to migrate next week schedule to current week and current week actual hours to the previous week. It will also reset current week actual hours and next week schedule back to 00:00. Some of the colors may not have enough contrast, if this is a concern, please reach out via the email link on the contact page. If there is enough demand to change the color scheme, we will put out a poll for users to vote on new colors.

This site is currently free to use. If there is a large amount of use, or if we go over the data allotments from the database provider, the site may not work. If that happens, we may upgrade to a paid service tier. The site would still be free to use but donations would be accepted to help offset costs. We would communicate this via email to all registered users. For more information, please feel free to reach out via the email link on the contact page. Thank you for your time and support.
